## What it is

Claude Opus 4.6 is the immediate predecessor to Opus 4.7 and remains an active high-end Claude option. Anthropic highlights it for agentic coding, knowledge work, search-heavy tasks, and sustained multi-step reasoning.

## Official system card in Markdown

This is a markdown digest of Anthropic's official system-card and launch material, not a verbatim copy.

### Capability and product summary

- Anthropic introduced Opus 4.6 as its smartest model at release time, with stronger planning, debugging, and large-codebase behavior than Opus 4.5.
- The official launch material emphasizes 1M-token context in beta, 128K-token outputs, adaptive thinking, and effort controls.
- Anthropic also tied the model to Claude Code agent teams and longer-running API task loops.

### Safety and deployment summary

- Anthropic says the full Opus 4.6 system card covers the most comprehensive safety evaluation set it had run for a Claude model at launch.
- The launch notes call out lower rates of misaligned behavior, low over-refusal rates, and added cybersecurity safeguards.
- Anthropic also notes premium long-context pricing behavior beyond 200K tokens in the original release materials.

### Integration notes

- Opus 4.6 remains relevant where teams have not yet migrated to 4.7.
- Check current deprecation status before new production adoption.
